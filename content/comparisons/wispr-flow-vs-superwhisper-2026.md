Two tools come up consistently among freelancers: **Wispr Flow** and **SuperWhisper**. Both run on Mac. Both use AI to transcribe speech into text. But they make very different trade-offs, and for certain freelancers, those differences matter a lot.

Both tools are Mac-only. If you're on Windows, neither applies.

## What Wispr Flow Does (And Why Freelancers Like It)

Wispr Flow is an AI dictation layer that sits on top of every app on your Mac. You activate it with a keyboard shortcut, speak, and it types directly into whatever field is active: Gmail, Notion, Slack, Google Docs, a client proposal in Pages, a Figma comment, or a browser text box.

That "types directly into any app" part is the real differentiator from older tools like Siri dictation or Google Voice. There is no paste step, no clipboard, no switching windows. You speak and the words appear exactly where your cursor is.

Wispr Flow also learns your writing style over time. After a few sessions, it adapts to your vocabulary, your common phrases, and your general tone. Reviewers consistently note that it handles technical jargon and industry-specific terms better than it did on first use.

The processing happens on Wispr's cloud servers. Your audio is sent to their infrastructure, transcribed, and returned as text. For most freelancers, this is not a problem. For some, it is a deal-breaker (more on that in the privacy section).

**Pricing:** $15/month with a 7-day free trial. No one-time purchase option exists.

**Platform:** Mac and iPhone only. No Windows app.

## What SuperWhisper Does (And How It Is Different)

SuperWhisper is built on OpenAI's Whisper model, which runs locally on your Mac. No audio leaves your device. You press a shortcut, speak, stop, and SuperWhisper transcribes and pastes the text.

The local processing is the defining feature. It means SuperWhisper works offline, it works with confidential audio, and it does not depend on Wispr's servers being up or your internet connection being stable.

The trade-off is a bit more friction. SuperWhisper has two main transcription modes:

- **Fast mode:** Uses a smaller Whisper model, transcribes in near real-time. Accuracy is good for clear speech but drops noticeably on accents, technical vocabulary, or background noise.
- **Ultra mode:** Uses a larger, more accurate Whisper model. Transcription happens after you finish speaking, not live. There is a processing pause of two to five seconds. Accuracy rivals the best cloud tools.

For long-form work like drafting a client proposal or writing a project scope, Ultra mode produces cleaner results. For quick emails and messages, Fast mode is fine.

**Pricing:** $12/month, or $99 as a one-time purchase. The one-time option is the best deal if you plan to use it for nine or more months.

**Platform:** Mac and iPhone only. No Windows app.

SuperWhisper has a free tier that includes a limited number of transcription minutes per month: useful for testing whether it fits your workflow before paying.

## Privacy: The Key Reason Freelancers Choose SuperWhisper

This section matters most if your client contracts include non-disclosure clauses.

When you dictate with Wispr Flow, audio is sent to Wispr's cloud servers for processing. Wispr has a privacy policy, but the audio still leaves your device and travels to their infrastructure. For most professional communication, this is not meaningfully different from sending an email or using Google Docs.

But if you work in legal services, financial consulting, medical services, or any context where client confidentiality is governed by contract or regulation, sending audio to a third-party cloud server is not acceptable. NDAs typically prohibit disclosing confidential information to third parties, and "third party" can include a transcription service's servers.

SuperWhisper processes everything locally. The audio never leaves your Mac. From a data handling standpoint, it is closer to writing in a local text editor than to using a cloud tool. This makes it the only serious option for freelancers who handle genuinely sensitive material.

If your NDA freelance contract has language about third-party disclosure, check with your client before using any cloud-based transcription tool. If you cannot get clarity, use SuperWhisper.

## Accuracy and Speed: Which Gets Your Words Right?

For short-form dictation, Wispr Flow has a slight accuracy edge. Its cloud model is tuned for conversational speech and handles filler word cleanup automatically. Reviewers consistently find that short emails, Slack messages, and social captions come out clean with minimal correction needed.

SuperWhisper Fast mode is close but not identical. Users on standard English report excellent accuracy. Users with regional accents or who use specialized vocabulary (medical, legal, technical) report slightly more errors in Fast mode.

SuperWhisper Ultra mode changes the calculation for longer content. For a 500-word project proposal or a long client email with detailed instructions, Ultra mode accuracy matches or exceeds Wispr Flow. The trade-off is the processing pause: you dictate a paragraph, pause, wait two to five seconds, and the text appears.

For freelancers who dictate long-form content regularly, Ultra mode is worth the pause. For those who mainly use dictation for quick responses and messages, the real-time nature of Wispr Flow is the better experience.

## Use Cases: Which App Wins for Specific Freelance Tasks

**Client email replies:** Wispr Flow. It types instantly into Gmail or your email client with zero context-switching. Best for the 10 to 15 emails a typical freelancer sends per day.

**Long project proposals:** SuperWhisper Ultra. Better accuracy on longer pieces, and a client proposal is exactly the kind of document you do not want to send through a cloud server.

**NDA-covered client work:** SuperWhisper. No question. Local processing is the only option.

**Quick social captions:** Wispr Flow. Speed matters more than accuracy for a 50-word Instagram caption, and Wispr Flow's real-time output is faster.

**Confidential financial or legal documents:** SuperWhisper. Same reason as NDA work.

**Video script outlines:** Either tool works. SuperWhisper Ultra produces slightly cleaner transcripts for longer dictation sessions.

**Commute dictation (iPhone):** Both have iOS apps. Wispr Flow's iPhone app has more reviews and is slightly more polished. SuperWhisper's iOS app is newer but functional.

## Verdict: Which Should You Use?

The answer depends on one question: do you handle confidential client work?

If yes, use SuperWhisper. The local processing is not a nice-to-have; it is a professional requirement for freelancers operating under NDA. The one-time purchase option also means you are not adding another monthly subscription.

If no, use Wispr Flow. It is the smoother, faster experience for everyday dictation. The learning-your-style feature improves over time. For client emails, Slack messages, social content, and quick project notes, it is the more frictionless tool.

Both have free options to test before paying. SuperWhisper's free tier gives you a real feel for the tool. Wispr Flow's 7-day trial is enough to know whether the workflow fits.
