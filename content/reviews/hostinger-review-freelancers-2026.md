## What you get for under $3/month

The Premium plan is where most freelancers land. Here is what is actually included and why each item matters for a freelance portfolio:

**100 GB SSD storage.** A typical freelance portfolio with 20 to 30 portfolio images, a blog, and a contact form uses under 2 GB. You will not come close to 100 GB with a normal client-facing site.

**Free domain for the first year.** Included on all annual plans. Pick your domain at signup. After year one, renewal is paid separately at standard registrar rates (typically 800 to 1,500 rupees per year). Don't buy the domain separately elsewhere before signing up, you'll miss the bundled discount.

**Free SSL.** SSL is now table stakes: browsers flag sites without it as insecure, and Google factors it into rankings. Hostinger includes Let's Encrypt SSL at no extra cost on all plans. It activates automatically after domain connection.

**Email accounts.** You can create professional email addresses at your domain (hello@yourdomain.com, contact@yourdomain.com) and either use them via webmail or forward them to your existing Gmail. For most freelancers, one forwarding address is all you need.

**Weekly automated backups.** The Premium plan includes weekly backups. Not daily, which is worth knowing. If you make a significant mistake on a Wednesday and the last backup was Saturday, you lose four days of changes. For a slow-moving portfolio site, weekly is fine. If you want daily backups, that's the Business plan.

**LiteSpeed web server.** LiteSpeed outperforms Apache (used by most budget hosts) at equivalent resource levels. This is why Hostinger's load times in third-party tests run faster than comparably priced hosts.

**AI website builder.** A drag-and-drop builder that generates a starting site from a text prompt. Useful if you want to launch without a WordPress learning curve. Less useful once you want a blog, custom landing pages, or anything beyond a basic five-page site.

What you don't get: staging environments, dedicated IPs, Git from the panel, or granular server control. For a portfolio site, you won't miss any of that.

## Setting up your portfolio site: step by step

{{< video-loop src="/img/ai/wordpress-install-concept.mp4" poster="/img/ai/wordpress-install-concept-poster.webp" caption="How a one-click WordPress install flows: click, progress, site live. Illustration." >}}

Hostinger's onboarding is among the smoother ones in the budget host category, based on what users consistently report across review platforms. Here is the actual flow from signup to live site.

**Step 1: Choose your plan and term at signup**

Premium is the right plan for most freelancers. Single limits you to one website with reduced resources. Business adds cost without meaningful benefit for a portfolio site. More important than the plan is the term: commit to the longest period you are comfortable with. A 48-month Premium plan at promo pricing costs significantly less per month than a 12-month plan at renewal rates.

**Step 2: Register or connect your domain**

If you don't have a domain, register one during signup using the included free domain offer. Pick a .com if it's available for your name. If you already own a domain from another registrar, point your existing domain to Hostinger's nameservers from your registrar's DNS panel. DNS propagation takes up to 48 hours, so plan accordingly if you have a launch date in mind.

**Step 3: Install WordPress from hPanel**

Go to hPanel, then Websites, then Install WordPress. The installer takes under 2 minutes. Choose your site language, set your admin username and a strong password, and confirm. You get a dashboard link immediately.

**Step 4: Install a fast, portfolio-ready theme**

The default WordPress theme is not suitable for a professional portfolio. Install one of these free themes: Astra, GeneratePress, or Kadence. All three are lightweight, fast, and include pre-built portfolio starter templates. Avoid bloated multipurpose themes with 50 features you won't use.

**Step 5: Build your five core pages**

A freelance portfolio site needs exactly five pages: Home, About, Services, Work (your portfolio gallery), and Contact. A clean five-page site with clear writing outperforms a complicated ten-page site every time. Use a contact form plugin (WPForms Lite is free and sufficient) on the Contact page.

**Step 6: Set up your professional email**

hPanel includes email accounts. Create hello@yourdomain.com and either use it directly via webmail or forward it to your Gmail using the email forwarding setting. Most freelancers prefer forwarding so all email arrives in one inbox.

**Step 7: Connect your domain and activate SSL**

If you registered your domain through Hostinger, the connection is automatic. If you brought your own domain, add Hostinger's nameservers at your external registrar. SSL activates automatically after domain connection but can take up to 24 hours to fully propagate.

The full process takes 2 to 3 hours including theme customisation and adding your portfolio content. Some freelancers complete it in under an hour with a minimal setup.

## Speed and uptime: what the data shows

Hostinger runs on LiteSpeed web servers, which gives it a speed edge over Apache-based budget hosts at the same price point. Data from GTmetrix and independent monitoring services (2025 to 2026) consistently shows:

- Average load time: 400 to 700ms for basic WordPress sites (varies by plan and region)
- Uptime: 99.9% claimed; independent monitoring generally confirms 99.8 to 99.95% in practice
- Nearest server to India: Singapore

For a portfolio with text, images, and a contact form, this is more than adequate. Clients won't notice a 600ms load time. Where you'd feel the limits is running WooCommerce with 100+ products or handling significant concurrent traffic, that's outside what the budget plans are built for.

## Customer support: the honest truth

Hostinger is in the middle of the pack here.

24/7 live chat is genuinely available and handles basic questions well. The knowledge base covers most common setup tasks with step-by-step articles, WordPress install, email setup, domain connection.

Where it gets inconsistent: users across Trustpilot, G2, and Reddit report that support quality varies depending on who you get. Simple questions resolve quickly. Complex issues, migrations, billing disputes, server-level problems can take longer and sometimes need escalation before you get a useful answer.

If you're building a straightforward portfolio, you're unlikely to run into the difficult edge of that. The knowledge base covers most use cases. But if you know you'll need regular technical support for custom configurations, Hostinger's support track record at this price point may frustrate you.
